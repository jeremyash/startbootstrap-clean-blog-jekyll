About

My academic background is in ecology and environmental science, with a specific focus on quantitative plant ecology. I am larely driven by research that uses robust quantitative methods to address ecological questions with applied conservation outcomes.

With each work and research experience, I have become more interested in how to better couple the ever increasing amount of available data with statistics and predictive modeling. For my master’s research, I combined my own field data collection with long-term demographic data to develop models that assess sustainability of leaf harvest from a palm spcies in Mexico. For my Ph.D. research, I am integrating several existing datasets with statistical models that seek to understand recent and future ecological impacts of climate change on plant species and communities in Wisconsin.
